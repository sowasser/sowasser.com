I am a marine conservation biologist in training, studying computational approaches to issues at the intersection of fisheries and climate change.

I am currently a PhD candidate at NUI Galway researching mackerel collective behaviour. I hold an MSc in Biodiversity & Conservation (Distinction) from Trinity College Dublin. I am the Student Representative to the Society for Conservation Biology Marine Section Board. I am also a tutor at the NUIG Academic Writing Centre, combatting writing fears one student at a time.

Previously, I lived in Iceland on a U.S. Fulbright Student Grant modelling the capelin spawning migration. Before that, I worked in the Turks & Caicos Islands as a waterfront intern with The School For Field Studies Center for Marine Resource Studies. I received a B.A. in Environmental Studies from Vassar College, where I wrote my undergraduate thesis on invasive lionfish, studied the swimming kinematics and schooling behaviour of cownose rays, and also worked at the writing center.

I am a PADI scuba instructor when the water is warm enough and inveterate tea-drinker when it isn't. Yay ocean!
